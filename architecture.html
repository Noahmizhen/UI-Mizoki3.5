Technical Architecture

Three subsystems. One governed decision loop.

MIZOKI3 is a ground-up proprietary decision intelligence platform — not a wrapper on third-party language models. The architecture comprises three integrated subsystems that every domain lens shares.

Subsystem 01

TCO-KG — Temporal-Causal Operational Knowledge Graph

A continuously evolving graph that models entities, relationships, obligations, events, and outcomes over time. Counsel maps covenants here. Capital reads liquidity position from here. Signal writes causal attribution edges here.

All domain lenses read from and write to this shared substrate — which is why what one lens learns, the others inherit without re-ingestion.

Subsystem 02

SRPVDAL — Seven-Stage Decision Pipeline

Every consequential decision flows through seven stages. Nothing skips a step. Nothing reaches execution without traversing the full loop.

  1. Sense — ingest and contextualize signals from domain sources
  2. Reason — traverse causal relationships in the graph
  3. Plan — generate constrained candidate actions
  4. Validate — stress-test against policy and counterfactuals
  5. Decide — select, defer, or escalate
  6. Act — execute authorized payloads
  7. Learn — update the graph from outcomes

Subsystem 03

DCP — Decision Control Plane

A non-bypass governance layer between agent proposals and system execution. Every action is scored for causal confidence, policy compliance, and risk before authorization.

When the Gate vetoes — as with ACT-991 — the decision includes the arithmetic, policy citation, and causal trace. Not a black-box refusal.
